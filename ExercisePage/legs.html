Squat-Smith-machine (1)
Do one of these
1

Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly turned out, and the barbell resting on your upper back. Engage your core and keep your chest up. The squat primarily targets the quads and glutes, but the rear delts help stabilize the barbell on your back throughout the movement.

2

Slowly lower your body by bending at the knees and hips, keeping your back straight and chest lifted. Engage your rear delts to help maintain balance and control as you descend into the squat, ensuring your knees don't pass too far beyond your toes.

3

Drive through your heels and stand back up, extending your hips and knees while keeping your rear delts engaged to maintain proper posture and shoulder stability. Fully extend your legs at the top, then repeat the movement for the desired number of reps.



Leg press (2)
Do one of these
1

For dumbbells, sit on a bench with back support, holding dumbbells at shoulder height with palms facing forward. For the machine, adjust the seat so the handles align with your shoulders. Both exercises target the rear deltoid muscles.

2

Press the dumbbells or machine handles upward, focusing on engaging the rear delts, until your arms are fully extended but not locked out.

3

Lower the weights back to the starting position in a controlled motion, ensuring the rear delts remain activated throughout. Keep your core engaged and maintain proper posture.

Hamsting (3)
Do one of these
1

Lie face down on the hamstring curl machine with your knees just off the edge of the bench and your feet under the padded bar. Adjust the machine so that the pad rests comfortably on your lower legs. The hamstring curl targets the hamstrings, with the rear delts activated as stabilizers to help maintain upper body control throughout the movement.

2

Curl your legs upward by bending your knees, focusing on engaging the hamstrings. Keep your rear delts active to prevent any arching of your back and ensure your torso remains stable during the movement.

3

Slowly lower the weight back to the starting position in a controlled manner, ensuring rear delts remain engaged to stabilize your upper body and prevent any unnecessary movement.



Calves (3)
Do one of these
1

Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart on the calf raise machine or on a raised platform with your heels hanging off. Keep your back straight and your chest up, ensuring proper posture. The calf raise primarily targets the calves, but the rear delts help maintain balance and stability throughout the movement.

2

Rise up onto the balls of your feet by extending your ankles, focusing on squeezing your calves at the top of the movement. Engage your rear delts to help stabilize your torso and maintain balance as you lift your heels.

3

Slowly lower your heels back to the starting position, maintaining control of the movement. Keep your rear delts engaged to ensure your posture remains stable and your upper body does not sway during the exercise.
